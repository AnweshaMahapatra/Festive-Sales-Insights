# 📊 Business Questions & Insights 🚀  
## Stakeholder Review AtliQ Mart Promotional Performance Analysis

*10 strategic questions grounded in the actual data from the Diwali 2023 and Sankranti 2024 campaigns.*

---

## Q1. 🏙️ Which state witnessed the highest increase in revenue and what explains its performance?

**Answer: Madurai at 120% IR% outperforming cities with far larger store networks.**

- 🥇 Madurai leads all 10 cities in IR% despite having only 4 stores  
- 📈 120% IR% means +20% revenue over baseline during promotions  
- 🏬 Chennai (116.84%) and Bengaluru (116.05%) are lower despite bigger networks  
- 🔍 Insight: performance is NOT driven by store count or scale  
- 🎯 Key reason: **strong promotional fit + better execution at store level**  
- ⚡ Action: replicate Madurai’s promo mix in underperforming cities like:
  - Mangalore (98.82%)
  - Visakhapatnam (94.39%)

---

## Q2. 💰 Which promotion resulted in the highest incremental revenue?

**Answer: 500 Cashback ₹91M IR (Diwali contributes ₹77M)**

- 🥇 Top revenue-generating promotion: 500 Cashback  
- 💸 Total IR: ₹91M across both campaigns  
- 🆚 Beats BOGOF (₹69M) despite lower volume  
- 📦 41K units vs BOGOF 372K units → much higher efficiency  
- 💡 Revenue per unit:
  - 500 Cashback ≈ ₹2,220/unit  
  - BOGOF ≈ ₹185/unit  
- 🧠 Why it works:
  - Customer sees benefit, but company retains revenue
  - Strong for premium categories like Home Appliances  

---

## Q3. ⚠️ Which promotion resulted in the lowest incremental revenue and should it be retired?

**Answer: 25% OFF at –₹3M IR yes, all discount promos need review.**

- ❌ 25% OFF: –₹3M IR (worst)
- ❌ 33% OFF: –₹2M IR  
- ❌ 50% OFF: –₹1M IR  
- 📉 All discount-based promotions are revenue-negative  
- 🧮 Why:
  - 33% discount → needs 50% more volume to break even  
  - 50% discount → needs 100% more volume  
- ❗ Reality: volume increase was NOT enough  
- ⚠️ Conclusion:
  - Don’t blindly use discounts
  - Re-evaluate depth + product fit + timing  

---

## Q4. 📦 Which product showed the highest lift in revenue and what does it tell us about the portfolio?

**Answer: Atliq Waterproof Immersion Rod 266.19% IR%**

- 🥇 Top product: Atliq Waterproof Immersion Rod (266.19%)  
- 🥈 Second: LED Bulb (262.98%)  
- 🏠 All top products belong to:
  - Home Appliances  
  - Home Care  
- 📊 Insight:
  - High promo sensitivity categories  
  - Customers respond strongly to offers  
- 🎯 Business implication:
  - Increase budget allocation to these categories  
  - Reduce focus on low-elasticity categories  

---

## Q5. 🧴 Which product showed the lowest lift and what does that mean for how we promote it?

**Answer: Personal Care category at –34.20% IR%**

- ❌ Only negative category: Personal Care (–34.20%)  
- 📉 ISU still positive (24.50%), but revenue dropped  
- 🧠 Meaning:
  - Customers already buy these products regularly  
  - Discounts only reduce revenue (no new demand created)  
- ⚠️ Problem: cannibalization of full-price sales  
- 🎯 Action:
  - Stop discount promotions here  
  - Use:
    - Loyalty points  
    - Bundles  
    - Non-discount offers

## Q6. 🎯 Between Diwali and Sankranti, which campaign should receive greater investment in future?

**Answer: Diwali — ₹89M IR vs ₹66M IR**

- 🥇 Diwali performs better overall  
- 💰 Higher incremental revenue: ₹89M vs ₹66M  
- 📦 Driven by:
  - 500 Cashback dominance (₹77M IR)  
- 🆚 Sankranti:
  - Driven by BOGOF (high volume, low efficiency)  
- 📊 Insight:
  - Diwali = revenue-efficient campaign  
  - Sankranti = volume-heavy campaign  
- 🎯 Action:
  - Increase Diwali-style promotions in future planning  

---

## Q7. 📦 BOGOF generated 372K incremental units is that success?

**Answer: Success depends on objective definition**

- 📦 Total ISU: 372K (highest among all promotions)  
- 🏬 Sankranti BOGOF alone: 313K units  
- 💰 IR: ₹69M (lower than 500 Cashback)  
- 🎯 Interpretation:

**If goal = volume:**
- ✅ BOGOF = best performer  

**If goal = revenue:**
- ❌ BOGOF = inefficient  

- 🧠 Key issue:
  - No clear objective definition before campaigns  
- ⚡ Fix:
  - Define campaign goal before launch:
    - Revenue → 500 Cashback  
    - Volume → BOGOF  
    - Clearance → targeted discounts  

---

## Q8. 📍 Bengaluru has 10 stores but only 116.05% IR%. Is that a problem?

**Answer: Not a failure but signals inefficiency at scale**

- 🏬 Bengaluru = largest store network (10 stores)  
- 📊 IR% = 116.05% (below Madurai & Chennai)  
- 🧠 Insight:
  - Percentage is lower, but absolute revenue likely higher  
- ⚠️ Concern:
  - Some stores underperforming (STBLR bottom list)  
- 🎯 Action:
  - Do NOT fix city-level  
  - Fix store-level performance issues  

---

## Q9. 🏠 Home Appliances showed 628.78% ISU% how should inventory planning change?

**Answer: Build pre-promotion inventory buffer**

- 📈 Demand spike: 628.78% ISU%  
- 🔥 Means: ~7× normal demand during promotions  
- ⚠️ Risks:
  - Stockouts (lost sales)  
  - Overstock (bad planning)  
- 🧠 Insight:
  - Demand pattern is consistent and predictable  
- 🎯 Action:
  - Pre-build inventory for:
    - Immersion Rod  
    - LED Bulb  
  - Especially before Diwali & Sankranti  

---

## Q10. 🧠 If only one change could be made to improve next campaign?

**Answer: Remove discount promos and shift budget to 500 Cashback**

- ❌ Remove:
  - 25% OFF → –₹3M  
  - 33% OFF → –₹2M  
  - 50% OFF → –₹1M  
- 💸 Total loss removed: ₹6M  
- ✅ Reallocate to:
  - 500 Cashback  
  - Home Appliances  
  - Home Care  
- 📊 Why:
  - Highest IR% across all data  
  - Strongest revenue-per-unit performance  
- 🧠 Key insight:
  - One structural change can fix campaign efficiency
